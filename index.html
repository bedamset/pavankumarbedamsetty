Research Project: Addressing the Skilled Labour Shortage in India's Construction Sector

Coventry University | MSc Construction Management with BIM | 2023
Supervisor: Mr. Sanjeev Lehil

“Addressing the lack of skilled workers on construction sites in India and proposing a strategic framework for bridging the divide.”

Overview

This postgraduate research investigated the national-scale challenge of skilled labour shortages in India’s construction sector — an industry contributing over £22 billion annually to the GDP and employing millions. The study identified root causes, assessed impacts on project performance, and proposed an evidence-based strategic framework to bridge the skills gap.

Methodology

  • Mixed-methods approach under a pragmatic research philosophy
  • Surveyed 85 construction professionals via Microsoft Forms
  • IBM SPSS statistical analysis to identify frequency patterns & correlations
  • Thematic analysis for qualitative insights into training, education, and technology adoption
  • Developed the original “Convergence Model” for methodological reliability and data integration

Key Findings

  • 85% of construction firms reported skilled worker shortages affecting productivity, cost, and safety
  • Training gaps, migration patterns, and limited adoption of digital tools (BIM, CSCS Cards) identified as primary causes
  • Correlation established between skill deficits and project quality decline
  • Proposed strategic workforce framework: continuous skill-assessment, industry-academia collaboration, youth apprenticeships, and technology-driven upskilling

Results & Recognition

  • Earned Distinction (73%) for research quality and practical relevance
  • Framework considered as a model for training standardisation and workforce digitalisation in developing contexts

Skills & Tools Gained

  • Quantitative & qualitative research, data modelling (SPSS, MS Forms)
  • Policy & workforce analytics
  • BIM & digital construction tools
  • Technical report writing, strategic framework development, stakeholder collaboration

Research Project: NDVI-Based Vegetation and Land-Use Analysis Using Remote Sensing Data

Coventry University | Department of Environment, Engineering, and Computing | 2023

“Applying satellite-based NDVI analysis to evaluate vegetation health, urban expansion, and land-use dynamics for sustainable development planning.”

Overview

This project explored the use of Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (NDVI) to measure vegetation health and assess land-use transformation in developing regions. The study integrated satellite imagery with GIS analytics to enable informed decision-making for sustainable urban and rural development.

Methodology

  • Quantitative remote-sensing approach using Sentinel-2 and Landsat-8 imagery (2013–2023)
  • Pre-processing and atmospheric correction with QGIS and ArcGIS Pro
  • NDVI computation across seasonal intervals to monitor vegetation health
  • Supervised classification of land-use categories (urban, agricultural, forest, barren)
  • Change detection analysis to quantify vegetation degradation or growth trends

Key Findings

  • Overall 12–15% decline in vegetation density in rapidly urbanizing areas
  • Agricultural land NDVI fluctuated ±0.35 seasonally, reflecting irrigation and monsoon effects
  • Forested zones maintained high NDVI (>0.65), showing ecological stability
  • GIS-based visualization dashboard created for policy and environmental reporting

Results & Impact

  • Provided scientific basis for urban planning and reforestation initiatives
  • Actionable insights for sustainable land management and climate-resilience policy
  • Demonstrated potential of remote sensing + GIS integration as scalable, low-cost environmental monitoring

Skills & Tools Gained

  • Remote Sensing, QGIS, ArcGIS Pro, ERDAS Imagine, Raster Analysis
  • Python (Rasterio, NumPy, Matplotlib) for geospatial data processing
  • Environmental data interpretation, report writing, sustainable urban planning insight
